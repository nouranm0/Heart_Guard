📈 Cardiac Parameters Analyzed

Heart Rate (HR)
Measured in beats per minute (bpm). Normal range: 60-100 bpm. Bradycardia (<60) or tachycardia (>100) detection.
Range: 30-200 bpm
P Wave
Represents atrial depolarization. Duration: 80-120ms. Abnormalities indicate atrial pathology or enlargement.
Duration: 40-120 ms
PR Interval
Measures AV nodal conduction time. Normal: 120-200ms. Prolongation suggests AV block; shortening may indicate pre-excitation.
Range: 120-200 ms
QRS Duration
Represents ventricular depolarization. Normal: <120ms. Widening indicates bundle branch block or ventricular pathology.
Normal: 60-120 ms
ST Segment
Baseline between QRS and T-wave. Elevation or depression indicates myocardial infarction or ischemia.
Reference: 0 mm baseline
QT Interval
Measures ventricular repolarization duration. Corrected QT (QTc) <440ms. Prolongation increases arrhythmia risk.
Corrected: <440 ms
T Wave
Represents ventricular repolarization. Inversion may indicate ischemia, myocarditis, or pulmonary embolism.
Amplitude: ±5 mm
Arrhythmia Detection
Identifies irregular rhythms: Atrial Fibrillation, Premature beats, Ventricular tachycardia, Heart blocks.

🏥 Clinical Application

The HEARTGAURD AI model serves as a clinical decision support tool, assisting physicians in screening large patient populations for cardiac abnormalities. Real-time flagging of high-risk patients enables prioritization for further specialist evaluation and intervention. The system is designed to complement, not replace, clinical judgment and standard diagnostic procedures.
